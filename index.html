About Me

I am a Michigan State 2021 graduate with a major in Experience Architecture (commonly referred to as User Experience). For me, technology is a creation by people to close a gap somewhere where there is a need. UX comes out of the need to put humans back into the design process as equal stakeholders in their experience. I, as a UXer, bring experiences to life as I tell the stories of humans in the way that matters to them. With my experiences both in and out of the classroom, I have used curiosity and empathy as a way to practice human-centered design. By being strategic in the information I collect and the way in which I collect it, I am able to solve my problems in a way I can later explain to other people and can easily be replicated across different mediums. This has served me well from serving customers in dining halls with fellow team members to working solo on developing a website.

In my free time, I can be found reading eBooks (I, unfortunately have not read a print book for leisure in years). A fun fact about me is that I can rarely re-read a book. Currently, the only book I have re read is "The Hunger Games" in which there was a 6 year gap. I also like watching movies and I watched the movies directly after, and then I realized I was annoyed the first time I watched the movies as some scenes didn't match up. Regardless, chances are I have watched at least one movie (not tv show, I never finish the first season) this week, so when you interview me, ask me about my latest movie!
